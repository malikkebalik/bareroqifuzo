Bill Belichick Early Life

William Stephen “Bill” Belichick was born on April 16, 1952, in Nashville, Tennessee. His parents, Jeannette and Steve, were assistant football coaches at the United States Naval Academy, and Bill’s godfather is Bill Edwards, who is in the College Football Hall of Fame. Bill grew up in Annapolis, Maryland. Belichick often accompanied Steve to coaches’ meetings and observed how his father analyzed the game films and devised plays. Bill was an integral part of the team’s practices from the time he was a teenager, and he quickly learned the game’s strategies and formations.

Belichick played football and lacrosse at Annapolis High School, where he also participated in the school’s academic program. After finishing high school in 1970, he spent a year at Phillips Academy in Massachusetts, where he boosted his standardized test scores and grade point average to enter Wesleyan University. While attending Wesleyan, Bill was a captain of the lacrosse team, a tight end and center for the football team, a squash player, and a member of the Chi Psi fraternity. Belichick earned his bachelor’s degree in economics back in 1975.

A linebackers coach since 1980, he previously worked in the same capacity in 1979 for the New York Giants.
