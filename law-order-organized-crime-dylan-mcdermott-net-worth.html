Dylan McDermott has enjoyed a successful acting career. He’s probably best known for playing Bobby Donnell on The Practice from 1997 to 2004. Now, he’s one of the main characters on Law & Order: Organized Crime. Here’s a look at McDermott’s career and net worth.

Dylan McDermott as Richard Wheatley on ‘Law & Order: Organized Crime’

McDermott plays bad guy Richard Wheatley on Law & Order: Organized Crime. He’s a pharmaceutical executive by day and a mob boss by night. Detective Elliot Stabler (Christopher Meloni) thinks Wheatley might have something to do with the murder of his wife, Kathy.

McDermott was fascinated when he first received the script for Law & Order: Organized Crime, reports Variety. He says he was reminded of his New York upbringing and the time he spent as a bartender.

“They sent me the script, and I initially thought that it was fascinating, you know, growing up in New York and being around these mobsters here and there,” McDermott tells Variety. “I remember when [Mafia figure] Joey Gallo got killed. My father and I went down to Umberto’s Clam House [where Gallo was shot] and looked around, and it was all cordoned off. And you know, I was a bartender, busboy and a waiter, so I’ve been around these guys a little bit.”

McDermott says he didn’t realize how popular the Law & Order franchise was until he signed on. “I was like, whoa, this is a world here, I had no idea how dedicated the fans are,” he tells Variety. “I mean, people love this show. It’s a comfort show. They watch the DVDs over and over again… I’ve never heard of such a thing.”

Dylan McDermott’s movies and TV shows

One of McDermott’s early acting roles was in the 1987 movie Hamburger Hill. The following year, he played Vince Holloway in The Blue Iguana.

McDermott rose to fame after appearing the 1993 film In the Line of Fire alongside Clint Eastwood. He played the role of Al D’Andrea. His other acting roles include appearances in The Practice, American Horror Story, Stalker, and Dark Blue.

Dylan McDermott’s work outside of acting

The Law & Order star has experience outside of acting. He also has credits as a director, producer, and writer. McDermott made his directorial debut with a 1999 episode of The Practice titled “Infected.” In 2017, he directed and produced the drama Sugar. He also has two soundtrack credits for performing “Wouldn’t It Be Nice” during Will & Grace Season 6 Episode 6, and “Two Princes” during LA to Vegas Season 1 Episode 7.

As of this writing, McDermott has an estimated net worth of $20 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth.
