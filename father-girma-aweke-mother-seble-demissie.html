Meet Naomi Girma Parents: Girma Aweke And Seble Demissie

Naomi Girma’s parents are Ethiopian immigrants. She is the daughter of Girma Aweke and Sebla Demissie and has one older brother, Nathaniel.

Girma’s parents came to the U.S. in their teens, and they met each other in the Bay Area. 

Naomi’s parents are not much active outside, but as an immigrant, her parents had to endure many struggles in their teens.

As per LifeBogger, Naomi’s father, Girma Aweke, graduated with an electrical engineering diploma after many struggles with jobs.

Similarly, Naomi’s mother, Sible Girma, also became a manager in the banking and finance sector after completing her education. Both of the parents can speak English and Amharic.

Naomi’s father was very interested in Football, and he formed Maleda Soccer Club in 2005 for the Bay Area Ethiopian community. Naomi started playing in that club and aimed to become a professional soccer player.

Naomi’s older brother is also interested in sports, and both siblings used to play Football together. 

Naomi has received much support and love from her family and her overall Ethiopian community, which is why she has scored the best results for her club and team at such an early age. 

Naomi Girma Career And Net Worth

Naomi Girma is one of the rising athletes, and as per Football Arroyo, she has an estimated net worth of $5 million.

As she started playing Football at her community Maleda Soccer Club, Naomi later joined Central Valley Crossfire Club in 2010. She also played for the California Thorns Academy and Pioneer High School.

Naomi became the Stanford Cardinal soccer team captain of Stanford University. And her team won the 2019 Women’s College Club. 

San Diego Wave FC selected Girma in the 2022 NWSLDraft. She was also named NWSL Best XI of the Month in May and June 2022. 

Furthermore, Naomi was named NWSL Rookie of the Month in June 2022, and later she was given the title of NWSL Defender of the Year and NWSL Rookie of the Year.

Naomi Sirma was also the captain of the United States under-20 teams. She made her senior national team debut on 12 April 2022. She also appeared in the 2022 CONCACAF W Championship with her team.

Moreover, Naomi was also selected as part of the US squad for the FIFA Women’s World Cup.
